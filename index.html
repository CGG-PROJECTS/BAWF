About this Database

  • Explore English word families, compound words, and definitions/etymologies
  • The British and American Word Families (BAWF) is a tool for vocabulary morphology instruction from the primary grades
  • Morphology is the study of how words are built from meaningful units, including bases, roots, prefixes, and suffixes (Hiebert, 2020). A child who understands that the prefix un- means not or reverse does not just know one word; she has a tool for working out the meanings of unhappy, undo, unfair, unknown, unusual, and dozens more. A student who recognizes the Latin root tract is often in words containing meanings such as ‘pull, draw’ is positioned to make more sense of attract, contract, distract, extract, retract, subtract – academic words that span multiple subject areas and grade levels (Coxhead, 2000).

Column Definitions for the BAWF Morphological Database

Core Word Information

  • Word: The surface wordform (as it appears in the dictionary and corpora)
  • Word_frq_Zipf: Zipf frequency score of the wordform, calculated from a large English corpus (higher value = more frequent in everyday language use).A Zipf value of 1 is approximately 1 occurrence per 100 million words; a value of 2 represents once per 10 million words; 3 once 1 per million words etc.
  • FLemma: The lemma of the word (ignoring part of speech).
  • GPT_Coded_Word_Parts: Morphological breakdown of the word into prefixes, root(s), and suffixes, generated by the fine-tuned GPT 4.1 analysis.

Baseword / Root Information

  • Baseword: The central base form of the word after removing affixes.
  • Baseword_frq_Zipf: Zipf frequency score of the baseword itself.
  • Baseword_Family_Size: Number of different wordforms that belong to the same baseword family (excluding compounds).
  • Baseword_FamilySize_Incl_Compounds: Number of different wordforms in the baseword family, including compounds.
  • Baseword_Zipf_Inc_Compounds: Zipf frequency of the baseword, adjusted to include compounds.

Root & Etymological Information

  • GPT_Root: The primary root identified using GPT-assisted morphological analysis.
  • Avg_Root_Zipf_BNC_COCA: Average Zipf frequency (based on BNC-COCA data) of words sharing the same root.
  • Root_FamilySize: Number of different wordforms sharing the same root (excluding compounds).
  • Root_FamilySize_Inc_Compounds: Number of different wordforms sharing the same root, including compounds.
  • Avg_Root_Zipf_Inc_Compounds: Average Zipf frequency of words sharing the root, including compounds.

Greek / Latin Roots

  • Greek_Latin_Root_1: First identified classical (Greek or Latin) root.
  • Greek_Latin_Meaning_1: Meaning of the first classical root.
  • Greek_Latin_Root_1_FamilySize (recompute): Number of different wordforms containing this classical root.
  • Avg_Greek_Latin_Root_1_Zipf_BNC_COCA: Average Zipf frequency of words containing the first classical root.
  • Greek_Latin_Root_2: Second identified classical root (if present).
  • Greek_Latin_Meaning_2: Meaning of the second classical root.
  • Greek_Latin_Roots_FamilySize_Incl_compounds: Total family size for classical roots, including compounds.
  • Greek_Latin_Roots_Zipf__Incl_compounds: Zipf frequency measures for classical roots, including compounds.

Prefixes & Suffixes

  • Prefix_1 to Prefix_3 and Suffix_1 to Suffix_6: Include meaning, all forms, frequency, count, and mean Zipf scores.

Other Morphological & Psycholinguistic Measures

  • N_Morphemes: Total number of morphemes in the word.
  • Reading AoA (Green et al., 2025): Age of Acquisition for reading this word.
  • Vocabulary eXpected Grade Level (Flor et al., 2024): Expected U.S. grade level.
  • GPT_Familiarity (Brysbaert et al., 2024): GPT-estimated familiarity rating.
