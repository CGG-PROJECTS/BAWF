Column Information Key – Easy Explanations

×

Core Word Information

  • Word: The actual word as you see it written (the “surface form”).
  • Word_frq_Zipf: Zipf score – a simple number that shows how common a word is in everyday English. Higher number = much more frequent.
  • FLemma: Lemma – the basic dictionary form of the word.
  • GPT_Coded_Word_Parts: How the word is broken into prefixes, root, and suffixes (done with AI help).

Baseword / Root Information

  • Baseword: The main root word after removing prefixes and suffixes.
  • Baseword_frq_Zipf: Zipf frequency score of the baseword itself.
  • Baseword_Family_Size: How many different words belong to the same baseword family (not counting compounds).

Root & Etymological Information

  • GPT_Root: The main root of the word (identified with AI help).
  • Avg_Root_Zipf_BNC_COCA: Average Zipf frequency of all words that share this root.

Greek / Latin Roots

  • Greek_Latin_Root_1 / Root_2: The classical (Greek or Latin) root(s) in the word.
  • Greek_Latin_Meaning_1 / Meaning_2: What that classical root actually means.

Other Useful Columns

  • N_Morphemes: Total number of meaningful parts (morphemes) in the word.
  • Reading AoA (Green et al., 2025): Estimated age at which children usually learn to read this word.
  • Vocabulary eXpected Grade Level (Flor et al., 2024): U.S. grade level when most students are expected to know this word.

Why Zipf scores are helpful: They let you quickly sort the whole database from the most common everyday words (high Zipf) to the rarest ones (low Zipf).

“Extended” = statistics that also include compound words (e.g. “blackboard”, “firefly”) that contain the same root or baseword.
